About Us
The Why

College campus sex crimes are in the national spotlight thanks to President Obama’s January 2014 memorandum. We already know sexual assaults are grossly underreported for many reasons, but we have even less knowledge about the non-physical sexual harassment offenses affecting college students every day.

And now sexual harassment has a new home: online. Cyberharassment is harassment in the form of emails, text messages, social media messages or any other form of electronic communication.

Sexual harassment is not a criminal offense, but cyberharassment is. It is referred to as telecommunications harassment in statutory law.

Little data have been collected that reflect the prevalence of cyberharassment on college campuses other than reports made to university police departments, so we decided to ask the students ourselves.

We have also gathered data that show the number of telecommunications harassment reports from other comparable public universities in Ohio. We have interviewed sexual violence experts, counselors, victims and law-enforcement agencies.


The goal

Our goal is to track the prevalence of sexual harassment and cyberharassment among the Kent State student body, share stories of victims and inform students about how they can prevent and put an end to cyberharassment.
