So, what is

cyberharassment?

Cyberharassment usually pertains to threatening or harassing email messages, instant messages or to blog entries or websites dedicated soely to tormenting an individual.

How do I know if I’m being cyberharassed?

If your situation meets the following criteria:
1. You have received unwanted phone calls, text messages, emails, social media messages, etc.
2. At any point in time, those messages were delivered without your consent.
3. The unwanted messages are pervasive (i.e. more than one).
4. The unwanted messages make you feel uncomfortable, threatened or offended.
5. The unwanted messages have had a negative effect on your daily life.

Criteria comply with the Kent State University Policy Register’s definition of harassment.

If you have been cyberharassed, Officer Michquel Penn of the Kent State Police Department says report it to the police:

  • Save all messages or records of calls.
  • Report the incident as quickly to collect as much evidence as possible.
  • Call the local or university police station.
  • Go to the station to make a statement, or have the officers come to you.

Our Mission and Goal

KnowHarassment provides an accurate representation of the prevalence of sexual harassment and cyberharassment within a student body by providing survey data from Kent State students. We investigate and report on what cyberharassment is, how to recognize it and how it can be halted. Our goal is to empower students across the county to end it.

So, how can you help?

Little data has been collected that reflects the prevalence of cyberharassment on college campuses, so we are asking students directly with a 3-minute, anonymous survey
