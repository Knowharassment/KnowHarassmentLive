So, what exactly is

harassment?

Harassment, often of a sexual nature, is a civil offense, meaning perpetrators cannot be criminally charged and punished. Cyberharassment, or telecommunications harassment in many states, is a criminal offense. Cyberharassment usually pertains to threatening or harassing email messages, instant messages, or to blog entries or websites dedicated soley to tormenting an individual.

Why should we care?

Our goal

KnowHarassment provides an accurate representation of the prevlence of sexual harassment and cyberharassment within a student body by providing survey data from Kent State students. We report on the ways cyberharassment differs from other sexual crimes in hope to empower students by showing how to recognize, properly handle and report it to law enforcement.

How can you help?

We've found that many people have been harassed and aren't aware of it. To solve this problem, we have created an annonymous, 5-minute survey.

KnowHarassment is a project developed by Kent State Student in the schools of JMC and DSCI.
